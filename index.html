Engagement Strategies by Funnel Stage
How leading brands win, activate, retain, and turn customers into advocates — and what actually doesn't work.
Awareness Activation Retention Loyalty
01
Stage 1
Awareness
Does the customer even know I exist?
Goal: capture attention and be remembered
Awareness Real-world examples
Spotify
Spotify Wrapped
Turns listening data into a personalized visual story users voluntarily share. The brand pays zero for distribution — the user distributes it for identity.
When content is personal enough, the user becomes your distribution channel.
Rappi · Colombia
Hyper-local Influencers
Activates neighborhood creators in Medellín and Cali showing the app in real everyday contexts — no actors, no studios, just the familiar corner store.
Hyper-local awareness converts 2-3× better than mass campaigns because cultural relevance is higher.
Duolingo
Mascot as Viral Character
The Duo owl became a global meme. Duolingo didn't create it — they adopted, amplified, and turned it into brand voice. Awareness without paid media, generated from culture.
Participating in popular culture with self-awareness generates organic awareness no budget can replicate.
Awareness What works vs. what doesn't
What works
  • ✓Personalized content users want to share
  • ✓Lookalike audience targeting
  • ✓Local cultural context — not generic global messages
  • ✓Joining existing conversations, not interrupting them
What doesn't work
  • ✗Talking to "everyone" with one message
  • ✗Measuring awareness only by paid impressions
  • ✗Generic content with no link to the user's life
  • ✗Ignoring the user's role as a distribution channel
02
Stage 2
Activation
Does the customer try and understand the value?
Goal: the "aha moment" — the instant value becomes real
Activation Real-world examples
Duolingo
Value before sign-up
Drops you into a lesson before asking you to create an account. The first exercise is short and satisfying — you feel the value before committing. Sign-up comes after the "aha".
Remove all friction before the first moment of value. Registration is friction — put it after, not before.
Notion
Templates as activation
Instead of a blank page, dozens of ready-made templates from day one. The user feels immediate productivity before understanding how the tool actually works.
Activation accelerates when the user sees a concrete result — not when they read a feature list.
Nu (Nubank) · Latam
Gamified onboarding
Gamifies banking onboarding with a progress bar and celebration at each step. Applying for a card feels like a personal achievement, not a bureaucratic form.
The perception of progress triggers the impulse to finish — users complete what they've already started.
Activation What works vs. what doesn't
What works
  • ✓Show value before asking for data or registration
  • ✓Onboarding adapted to the declared profile or use case
  • ✓Visible progress — the user knows where they are and what's left
  • ✓First concrete result in under 5 minutes
What doesn't work
  • ✗Sign-up + email verification + T&C before showing anything
  • ✗Long tutorials that explain instead of demonstrate
  • ✗Generic onboarding identical for all user profiles
  • ✗Asking for too much information from the start
03
Stage 3
Retention
Does the customer come back?
Goal: build habit and reduce churn
Retention Real-world examples
Duolingo
Streaks & Loss Aversion
The daily streak turns learning into a habit. Break it and the owl reacts. Duolingo intentionally exploits the fact that losing hurts more than winning satisfies.
Habit-based retention lasts longer than incentive-based. Discounts create customers who wait for deals; habits create users who can't leave.
Amazon Prime
The ecosystem that makes leaving hard
Prime retains with video, music, reading, storage, and bundled discounts. Each extra service the customer uses raises the cost of leaving. Retention is product architecture, not a separate program.
The right customer retained with the right benefit stack generates exponentially higher CLV. Not everyone deserves the same effort.
Bancolombia
Proactive Re-engagement
Detects inactivity signals (no transactions in 30 days) and triggers personalized outreach based on real financial profile — not generic messages, but concrete relevant offers.
Re-engagement must happen before the customer decides to leave. Once they start evaluating alternatives, the cost of retaining them rises dramatically.
Retention What works vs. what doesn't
What works
  • ✓Designing habits — frequent use cycles built into the product
  • ✓Proactive re-engagement based on behavior signals
  • ✓Relevant communication based on CLV and customer profile
  • ✓Raising switching costs with real accumulated value
What doesn't work
  • ✗Discounts as the primary strategy — trains customers to wait for offers
  • ✗Reactive re-engagement — acting only after the customer has left
  • ✗Same retention effort for all segments
  • ✗Generic notifications that add no real value
04
Stage 4
Loyalty
Does the customer defend and recommend me?
Goal: turn customers into active brand advocates
Loyalty Real-world examples
Starbucks
Rewards as identity ecosystem
Starbucks Rewards isn't a points program — it's an identity. Drink personalization, stars, birthday gifts, exclusive launches. It combines transaction, personal ritual, and tribal belonging.
The loyal customer doesn't just buy more — they recommend, defend the brand, and forgive mistakes that would drive other customers away.
Apple
Community as product
No traditional points program — an ecosystem that generates identity. "Being an Apple user" carries tribe, aesthetic values, and a shared vocabulary. Loyalty isn't bought with perks, but with accumulated personal meaning.
The most durable loyalty is not rational but identity-based. Customers tolerate premium prices when the brand is part of their self-perception.
Tigo · Colombia
Programmatic Advocacy
Referral program with mutual incentives built into the app. The referring customer earns real benefits, and so does the new one. Reduces the effort it takes to recommend.
Advocacy doesn't happen spontaneously — it must be facilitated. A good program eliminates the friction of recommending.
Loyalty What works vs. what doesn't
What works
  • ✓Experiential loyalty programs, not just transactional ones
  • ✓Individual recognition that goes beyond purchase volume
  • ✓Facilitating advocacy — reducing the friction of recommending
  • ✓Co-creation with the most engaged customers
What doesn't work
  • ✗Measuring loyalty only by purchase volume
  • ✗Points programs with no emotional or experiential value
  • ✗Treating all "frequent" customers the same
  • ✗Expecting advocacy to happen on its own, without enabling it
Key Takeaways
The funnel is one continuous conversation
Each stage builds on the last. Winning at one without investing in the next is leaking value at every step.
Awareness
Make the user your distribution channel — personalization is more powerful than media spend.
Activation
Deliver the "aha moment" first. Every second of friction before value is a lost user.
Retention
Design habits, not discounts. Re-engage proactively — before they start looking elsewhere.
Loyalty
Build identity, not points. Make advocating easy — advocacy doesn't happen on its own.
